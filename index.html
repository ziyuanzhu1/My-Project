What is EDM?

Often abbreviated to EDM, electronic dance music boils down to "all music produced electronically for people to dance to". Keeping the above definition in mind, we can trace the origin of electronic dance music back to Jamaica in the 1960s. The artists there tried to create new forms of music by overlapping multiple tracks on reel-to-reel audio tape recorders. This certain style of music, which was called dub music, became popular in night clubs and bars and was essentially the first form of electronic dance music, even preceding disco. Within a next few decades, countless new genres popped up within the electronic dance music spectrum. And now, EDM is one of the most thriving and popular entities in the entire music landscape.
